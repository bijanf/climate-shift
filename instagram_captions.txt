Today is Sizdah Bedar — the 13th day of the Persian New Year and Nature Day. A day when Iranians celebrate the beauty of the earth and spend the day outdoors, showing gratitude to mother nature.

But since the Industrial Revolution, we have been pushing our planet out of its safe corridor of stability — toward the edge of its tipping points. The data below shows what that looks like across Europe.

Hope for better times — for both nature and humanity.

—

Europe's climate is splitting apart.

Slide 1: Up to 30 more days above 30 °C each summer in the Mediterranean.
Slide 2: More extreme downpours across Northern Europe.
Slide 3: More rainless days drying out the south.

All maps compare 1995–2024 to 1950–1979 using daily observations from over 27,000 weather stations (E-OBS v32, Cornes et al. 2018, 0.25° resolution).

2024 was Europe's hottest year on record (Copernicus ESOTC 2024). The past three years — 2024, 2023, 2025 — were the three hottest ever recorded globally.

References:
• Cornes et al. (2018), Int. J. Climatol. — doi.org/10.1002/joc.5264
• Copernicus (2025), European State of the Climate 2024 — climate.copernicus.eu/esotc/2024
